# VELOCUE Website Brand Foundation

Last updated: 2026-03-19
Scope: Website positioning and brand voice foundation for the marketing site

## Working decisions locked for current phase

- Primary promise: improve pre-ride preparation quality
- Secondary value: support better ride execution and performance decisions
- Current market: Traditional Chinese users in Taiwan
- Current website language: Traditional Chinese first
- English positioning and international messaging are out of scope for now

## 1. Product read, based on current project

From the current codebase, VELOCUE is not a general cycling dashboard. It is closer to a route interpretation tool for cyclists:

- Import a GPX route
- Visualize route map and elevation profile
- Add route notes at distance-based points
- Convert route understanding into a printable cue strip
- Help riders anticipate climbs and key moments before the ride

This means the current product value is not "tracking what happened after the ride".
It is "helping riders read the route before and during the ride".

## 2. Product positioning

### Product lineage

VELOCUE should be understood as an evolution of the same utility mindset behind `stem-pace / print_ct_route`.

That earlier workflow focuses on taking a pacing sheet made elsewhere, restyling it, printing it, and attaching it to the bike as a practical ride note.

VELOCUE extends that logic upstream:

- take the event or race route
- inspect the map and elevation together
- decide what matters before the ride
- place support notes onto the route itself
- export the result into a usable on-bike format

So the product is not a generic route viewer.
It is a route-based planning note tool for serious riding.

### One-line definition

VELOCUE is a route briefing and planning tool for serious cyclists that turns route files into clear, rideable notes.

### Short positioning statement

VELOCUE helps road, endurance, and race-focused cyclists plan a route before they ride it by turning GPX files into elevation context, route notes, and ride-ready outputs.

### Expanded positioning

VELOCUE sits between route discovery and ride execution.
It is not trying to replace a full cycling platform, training analytics suite, navigation system, or bike computer UI.
Its role is to make a route legible:

- where the hard parts begin
- how the elevation behaves
- where to place support, rest, or meetup decisions
- how to allocate effort before key climbs
- what to remember at key distances
- what is worth carrying onto the bike as a compact ride cue

### Category framing

Recommended category:
`Cycling route briefing tool`

Acceptable supporting descriptors:

- `Route planning note tool for cyclists`
- `Elevation and cue planning workflow`
- `Pre-ride route briefing companion`
- `GPX-based briefing tool for road and endurance riding`

Categories to avoid for the website:

- `cycling dashboard`
- `training analytics platform`
- `navigation app`
- `route recorder`

Those categories push the product into expectations the product should not lead with.

## 3. Core user value

VELOCUE should be framed around preparation quality, not data volume.

### Functional value

- Turn route files into understandable ride information
- Surface elevation and distance context quickly
- Let riders place support, pacing, meetup, and rest reminders onto the route
- Help riders make effort decisions before decisive sections
- Produce a practical output riders can bring onto the bike

### Emotional value

- Raise the quality of pre-ride preparation
- Replace vague route anxiety with prepared confidence
- Help riders feel more deliberate, composed, and performance-ready

### Transformation

From:
`I have the route, but I still do not know how to prepare for it.`

To:
`I know where to manage effort, where to plan support, and what I need to remember on the bike.`

## 4. Brand role for the website

The website should sell a feeling of prepared, capable riding, not a feeling of technical complexity.

The site should present VELOCUE as:

- thoughtful
- clear
- warm
- composed
- cyclist-native
- performance-aware

The site should not present VELOCUE as:

- data-heavy
- macho race-tech
- hyper-rugged
- gadget-first
- dashboard-like

## 5. Draft brand personality

### Brand character

VELOCUE is the calm route strategist who has already studied the course.

It does not shout. It does not overhype. It quietly helps riders arrive better prepared and make better decisions.

### Personality sliders

- Warm over clinical
- Refined over aggressive
- Assured over excited
- Practical over clever
- Spacious over dense
- Human over technical
- Competitive over casual

### Tension to preserve

The brand should feel rounded and welcoming, but never cute, toy-like, or casual to the point of softness.

This is important because your stated preference includes rounded display typography and warm sunlight tones.
Without guardrails, that direction can drift into lifestyle-cute instead of capable-premium.

## 6. Brand voice

### Voice principles

1. Clear first
Every sentence should help riders understand what VELOCUE does and why it matters.

2. Calm confidence
Use direct, unhurried language. Avoid urgent, loud, or over-optimized marketing phrases.

3. Sensory but controlled
The website can evoke terrain, rhythm, sunlight, and the feeling of being prepared, but should not become poetic to the point of vagueness.

4. Product-aware
Copy should stay tied to route understanding, planning, cueing, and ride preparation.

5. Specific over generic
Use route, climb, distance, elevation, cue, support, pacing, and note when they clarify meaning.
Avoid filler phrases like "unlock your ride potential".

### Tone keywords

- warm
- clean
- composed
- capable
- precise
- generous
- focused

### Tone to avoid

- startup-hype
- sports-tech macho
- overfriendly cute
- enterprise software
- quantified-self jargon

## 7. Messaging boundary: marketing vs product UI

This distinction should stay stable across the project.

### Website marketing language

Purpose:
Create desire, trust, and understanding.

Language shape:

- outcome-led
- lower density
- more emotional framing
- more narrative rhythm
- more selective detail

Examples:

- `Read the route before you ride it.`
- `Turn route files into clear decisions before race day.`
- `Know where the climb changes, where support matters, and what to remember on the road.`

### Product UI language

Purpose:
Help users complete tasks accurately.

Language shape:

- literal
- compact
- instructional
- unambiguous
- task-oriented

Examples:

- `Upload GPX`
- `Add note at 24.6 km`
- `Export cue strip`
- `Elevation profile`

### Rule

Do not put product UI labels directly into hero-level marketing copy unless they have been rewritten into benefit language.

## 8. Positioning recommendation

At this stage, the strongest positioning direction is:

`VELOCUE helps serious cyclists turn route files into preparation.`

This works because it connects:

- the actual product mechanics: GPX, elevation, notes, cue strip
- the real user outcome: better pre-ride decisions and better ride execution
- the desired website tone: warm, modern, spacious, composed

### Current messaging priority

For the current website phase, headline and upper-page messaging should prioritize:

`提升騎前準備品質`

Performance support should remain present, but should not lead the brand story.
It works best as the second layer:

- 幫助你更早做出配力判斷
- 幫助你在關鍵路段少一點猶豫
- 幫助你把準備轉成更穩定的騎乘執行

### Language and audience rule

For the current portfolio-building phase, the website should be written for Traditional Chinese users first.

This means:

- Chinese should be the source language, not a translation layer
- Copy rhythm should feel native to Taiwanese cyclists
- Terminology should prefer familiar local usage over direct English borrowing when possible
- English naming may remain in product or brand identifiers, but the website narrative should not depend on English copy

## 9. Current ambiguities to resolve next

These are the main open questions I see from the codebase and your stated preferences:

1. What exact rider label should Traditional Chinese copy use most often: `公路車騎士`, `耐力騎乘者`, `參賽者`, or a hybrid?
The product direction is clear, but the wording still affects tone.

2. Which local wording feels most natural for `route briefing` in Chinese?
This will affect the naming of the homepage concept and section headers.

3. How much should the website reference races and events explicitly versus staying one level more universal?
This affects imagery, examples, and the emotional tone.

4. What should be the primary example workflow on the homepage:
support planning, climb effort planning, meetup coordination, or cue strip output?
This determines the first proof block.

## 10. Working direction for the next phase

Unless future discussion changes it, the next design work should assume:

- VELOCUE is a route briefing tool, not a cycling dashboard
- The website leads with preparation quality and route readability
- The emotional frame is calm confidence with competitive intent, not intensity theater
- The visual language should feel sunlit, spacious, rounded, and disciplined
- Product UI and marketing voice must remain intentionally separate
- Cue strip is the current hero output, but the category should remain broader than cue strip alone
- Traditional Chinese is the primary content language for this phase
